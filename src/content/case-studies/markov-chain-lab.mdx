## Overview

Markov Chain Lab provides a hands-on environment for exploring discrete-time Markov chains, aimed at students and practitioners who want to verify theoretical results visually.

## Technical Approach

The steady-state distribution is computed via eigendecomposition of the transition matrix transpose. The eigenvector corresponding to eigenvalue 1 is extracted and normalized.

For absorbing chains, the canonical form is used to extract the fundamental matrix and compute absorption probabilities.

## Implementation Notes

State transitions are represented as a NumPy matrix. NetworkX builds the graph and Matplotlib renders it, with edge weights proportional to transition probabilities.
